  1. LogEI vs log(EI) clean-up — 05_acqf.tex / 06_acqf_opt.tex                                                     
  The paper explicitly separates two things that look identical in the slides:                                     
  - LogEI (Hutter 2009): expected improvement on the original scale when the surrogate is trained on               
  log-transformed $y$ → closed form with log-normal moments.                                                       
  - log(EI) (Ament 2023): the logarithm of EI, used purely for numerical stability.                                
  06_acqf_opt.tex currently only references Ament. A tiny remark contrasting the two would remove a common         
  confusion.

  08_parallel_bo.tex — Kriging Believer / Fantasizing slide (lines 64-70)                                             
                                                                                                                      
  The "Constant liar" bullet has an internal contradiction:                                                           
  Constant liar: fixed value (e.g., $\cmin$ or $\surro(\confI{1}) + c\sh(\confI{1})$)                                 
  - "Fixed value" is the defining property of Constant Liar (Ginsbourger 2010: CL[max], CL[mean], CL[min] are         
  constants chosen up-front).                                                                                         
  - The second example $\surro(\confI{1}) + c\sh(\confI{1})$ is not a fixed value — it depends on the candidate       
  $\confI{1}$. That's an LCB-style pessimistic fantasy, not a constant liar. Either drop that example or label it as a
   separate variant.                                                                                                  
                                                                                                 
  Also, c here clashes with the \cost macro convention (and earlier you specifically asked to switch c → β in         
  07_noisy.tex for the same kind of risk-aversion factor). Should probably be β here too.
